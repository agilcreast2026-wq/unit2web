Impressionist Artists

The Impressionist movement began in late 19th-century France and focused on capturing light, color, and everyday moments rather than realistic detail. Artists used loose brushstrokes and painted scenes quickly to show how things looked in a specific moment. Famous Impressionists often painted landscapes, city life, and leisure activities outdoors.

Claude Monet

Mike Prior/Redferns/Getty Images

Claude Monet was a French painter and one of the founders of the Impressionist movement. He is best known for his paintings of nature, especially his water lilies, gardens, and landscapes, which focused on light, color, and atmosphere rather than fine detail. Monet often painted the same scene multiple times at different times of day to capture changing light and mood.


Edgar Degas

Image: Public Domain / Picryl

Edgar Degas was a French Impressionist artist best known for his paintings and sculptures of dancers, ballet rehearsals, and everyday Parisian life. Unlike many Impressionists, he often worked indoors and focused on movement, unusual angles, and detailed human figures. His art captured moments of motion and modern city life with soft colors and expressive brushwork.
